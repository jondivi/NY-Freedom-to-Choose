About

With every crochet stitch, the Freedom to Choose mural amplifies the protests against the Supreme Court decision that overturned American rights to bodily autonomy and reproductive freedom. Artist Jen LaMastra's vision is to create a mural for each state, and craftivists across the country are wielding their crochet hooks to answer her call.

Just as the Statue of Liberty's torch lights our way to freedom, the mural transforms her torch into a caduceus topped with a uterus. The three pink stars represent the three dissenting Supreme Court Justice votes and the large triangle represents a uterus.

The mural is created from forty yarn squares, each hand crocheted by a New Yorker.

As Lady Liberty is the primary feature of Jen's mural, the New York mural warrants going big, and we have some big dreams. In January 2023, the 50th anniversary of the original Roe v. Wade decision, we will unveil the New York mural. Roe may have been struck down, but the fight continues!

The New York mural is just one of many Freedom to Choose murals being created all over the country. Craftivists in [insert finished mural states here] have already completed murals with more underway.
